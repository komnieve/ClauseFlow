    (a) "Buyer" means Acme Aerospace Corporation
    (b) "Supplier" means Precision Wire Systems, Inc.
    (c) "Government" means the United States Government
    (d) "Prime Contract" means Contract FA8615-23-C-0042

1.3 ACCEPTANCE OF ORDER

This Purchase Order constitutes an offer by Buyer to Supplier. Supplier's
acceptance of this offer is expressly limited to the terms and conditions
contained herein. Commencement of performance or shipment of Goods shall
constitute acceptance of all terms and conditions. Any additional or different
terms proposed by Supplier are hereby rejected unless expressly agreed to in
writing by Buyer's authorized procurement representative.

1.4 CHANGES

Buyer may at any time, by written notice, make changes within the general
scope of this Purchase Order in any one or more of the following:

    (a) Drawings, designs, or specifications
    (b) Method of shipment or packing
    (c) Place of delivery
    (d) Delivery schedule
    (e) Quantity (increases or decreases of up to 10%)

If any such change causes an increase or decrease in the cost of, or the
time required for, performance of any part of the Work, Buyer shall make
an equitable adjustment in the Purchase Order price or delivery schedule,

the Disputes clause. Nothing in this clause shall excuse Supplier from
proceeding with the Purchase Order as changed.

1.5 STOP WORK ORDER

Buyer may, at any time, by written order to Supplier, require Supplier to
stop all, or any part of, the Work called for by this Purchase Order for a
period of ninety (90) days after the order is delivered to Supplier, and
for any further period to which the parties may agree. Upon receipt of the
Stop Work Order, Supplier shall immediately comply with its terms and take
all reasonable steps to minimize the incurrence of costs allocable to the
Work covered by the order during the period of work stoppage. Within the
period specified, Buyer shall either:

    (a) Cancel the Stop Work Order; or
    (b) Terminate the Work covered by the order as provided in the
        Termination clause.

1.6 TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE

Buyer may terminate this Purchase Order, in whole or in part, at any time
by written notice to Supplier when it is in Buyer's best interest. Upon
receipt of a termination notice, Supplier shall immediately stop Work as
directed in the notice and notify its suppliers and subcontractors of the
termination. Supplier shall be entitled to an equitable adjustment for Work
performed prior to termination, including a reasonable profit, but in no
event shall Supplier's total recovery exceed the Purchase Order price.

1.7 TERMINATION FOR DEFAULT

Buyer may, by written notice of default to Supplier, terminate this Purchase
Order in whole or in part if Supplier:

    (a) Fails to deliver the Goods within the time specified;
    (b) Fails to make progress so as to endanger performance; or
    (c) Fails to perform any other provision of this Purchase Order.

Before terminating for default, Buyer shall provide Supplier written notice
specifying the failure and allowing ten (10) days to cure such failure. If
this Purchase Order is terminated for default, Buyer may require Supplier
to transfer title and deliver to Buyer any completed Goods, work in process,
and materials. Supplier shall be liable for any excess costs incurred by
Buyer in procuring substitute Goods.

All wire harness assemblies shall be manufactured in accordance with:

    (a) IPC/WHMA-A-620 Class 3 (Space and Military/High Performance

    (b) SAE AS50881 (Wiring Aerospace Vehicle)
    (c) Drawing requirements and specifications referenced on the

Supplier shall maintain personnel certified to IPC/WHMA-A-620 for all

2.6 SPECIAL PROCESS REQUIREMENTS

The following special processes require approval by Buyer or the
Government prior to use. Supplier shall not change approved special
process sources without prior written authorization from Buyer.

    (a) Crimping and Termination - Per NASA-STD-8739.4
    (b) Soldering - Per IPC J-STD-001 Class 3 with Space Addendum
    (c) Wire Marking - Per MIL-STD-202, Method 215
    (d) Potting and Encapsulation - Per manufacturer specifications
    (e) Environmental Sealing - Per AS81714 (connector) and
        MS27488 (backshell)
    (f) Shielding Termination - Per drawing requirements

All special processes shall be performed by Nadcap-accredited processors
or by Supplier if Supplier maintains internal Nadcap accreditation for
the applicable process.

2.7 NONCONFORMING MATERIAL

Supplier shall not ship nonconforming material without prior written
authorization from Buyer. Requests for deviation or waiver shall be
submitted through Buyer's Supplier Quality Portal and shall include:

    (a) Description of the nonconformance
    (b) Root cause analysis
    (c) Corrective action to prevent recurrence
    (d) Proposed disposition (use-as-is, rework, repair, scrap)
    (e) Technical justification for use-as-is or repair dispositions

Supplier shall segregate and identify all nonconforming material pending
disposition instructions. Nonconforming material shipped without
authorization may be returned at Supplier's expense and may result in
corrective action or removal from Buyer's Approved Supplier List.

2.8 CORRECTIVE ACTION

Supplier shall maintain a Corrective Action system conforming to AS9100
requirements. Upon notification of a quality escape, Supplier shall:

    (a) Respond with containment actions within twenty-four (24) hours
    (b) Submit root cause analysis within ten (10) business days
    (c) Submit corrective action plan within fifteen (15) business days
    (d) Implement corrective actions per the approved plan
    (e) Provide objective evidence of effectiveness

Failure to respond to corrective action requests in a timely manner may
result in assessment of liquidated damages, placement on Buyer's Supplier
Watch List, or removal from the Approved Supplier List.

                    SECTION 3: MATERIAL AND TRACEABILITY
================================================================================

3.1 MATERIAL CERTIFICATION

All materials used in the manufacture of Goods shall be traceable to the
original material manufacturer. Supplier shall obtain and maintain
Certificates of Conformance (C of C) or Certified Test Reports (CTR) for
all materials.

The following materials require full Certified Test Reports per the
applicable material specification:

    (a) Wire and cable per MIL-DTL-27500 or M22759
    (b) Connectors per AS39029 series
    (c) Contacts per MIL-DTL-39029
    (d) Heat shrink tubing per MIL-DTL-23053
    (e) Solder per J-STD-006

Material certifications shall be retained for a minimum of ten (10) years
from date of delivery and shall be provided to Buyer upon request.

3.2 COUNTERFEIT PARTS PREVENTION

Supplier shall maintain a Counterfeit Parts Prevention Program in
accordance with AS6174 and AS6496. Supplier shall:

    (a) Procure parts only from Original Component Manufacturers (OCM),
        Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEM), or authorized distributors
    (b) Maintain approved supplier controls including verification of
        authorized distributor status
    (c) Perform incoming inspection and testing appropriate to detect
        counterfeit parts
    (d) Maintain traceability of parts to their source
    (e) Report suspected counterfeit parts to Buyer and GIDEP within
        sixty (60) days of discovery

Use of independent distributors or brokers requires prior written
approval from Buyer and additional testing per AS6171.

3.3 LOT TRACEABILITY

Supplier shall maintain lot traceability for all materials used in Flight
Critical assemblies. Each deliverable unit shall be traceable to:

    (a) Raw material lot numbers and certifications
    (b) Manufacturing date codes
    (c) Personnel performing critical operations
    (d) Inspection and test records
    (e) Special process certifications

Lot traceability records shall be retained for the life of the aircraft
program plus three (3) years.

3.4 SHELF LIFE AND AGE CONTROL

Supplier shall implement controls to ensure that age-sensitive and
shelf-life limited materials are used within their specified life limits.
Materials shall be stored per manufacturer recommendations. The following
items are subject to shelf life control:

    (a) Solder paste and flux
    (b) Adhesives and sealants
    (c) Potting compounds
    (d) Heat shrink materials with adhesive
    (e) Wire insulation (per manufacturer)

Shelf life documentation shall be maintained and provided with shipment
for any materials incorporated with remaining shelf life requirements.

3.5 ROHS AND REACH COMPLIANCE

Unless otherwise specified on the drawing, all materials shall be
compliant with:

    (a) EU RoHS Directive 2011/65/EU and amendments
    (b) EU REACH Regulation (EC) No. 1907/2006

For military and aerospace applications where RoHS exemptions apply,
Supplier shall document the applicable exemption and provide material
declarations upon request.
