                    SECTION 1: GENERAL PROVISIONS
================================================================================

1.1 ORDER OF PRECEDENCE

In the event of any inconsistency between the provisions of this Purchase
Order, the inconsistency shall be resolved by giving precedence in the
following order:

    (a) The Schedule (Line Items and Delivery Requirements)
    (b) Special Contract Requirements (this Purchase Order)
    (c) Quality Requirements (Section 2)
    (d) SDC Specifications referenced herein
    (e) FAR/DFARS Clauses incorporated by reference
    (f) General Provisions (this Section 1)
    (g) Supplier's quotation dated December 5, 2024

1.2 DEFINITIONS

As used throughout this Purchase Order:

    (a) "Buyer" or "SDC" means Stellar Dynamics Corporation
    (b) "Supplier" means Apex Precision Manufacturing, Inc.
    (c) "Government" means the United States Government
    (d) "Prime Contract" means Contract N00019-24-C-0108
    (e) "Goods" means the items described in the Line Items section
    (f) "Flight Critical" means items whose failure could result in loss
        of aircraft or endangerment of crew
    (g) "Mission Essential" means items whose failure would degrade
        mission capability but not endanger the aircraft or crew

1.3 ACCEPTANCE OF ORDER

This Purchase Order constitutes an offer by Buyer to Supplier. Supplier's
acceptance is expressly limited to the terms and conditions contained
herein. Commencement of performance or shipment of Goods shall constitute
acceptance of all terms and conditions. Any additional or different terms
proposed by Supplier are hereby rejected unless expressly agreed to in
writing by Buyer's authorized procurement representative.

1.4 CHANGES

Buyer may at any time, by written notice, make changes within the general
scope of this Purchase Order in any one or more of the following:

    (a) Drawings, designs, or specifications
    (b) Method of shipment or packing
    (c) Place of delivery
    (d) Delivery schedule
    (e) Quantity (increases or decreases of up to 15%)

If any such change causes an increase or decrease in the cost of, or the
time required for, performance, Buyer shall make an equitable adjustment
in the Purchase Order price or delivery schedule, or both. Any claim by
Supplier for adjustment must be asserted within twenty (20) days from the
date of receipt of the written change notice.

1.5 TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE

Buyer may terminate this Purchase Order, in whole or in part, at any time
by written notice to Supplier when it is in Buyer's interest. Upon receipt
of a termination notice, Supplier shall immediately stop Work as directed
and notify its sub-tier suppliers of the termination. Supplier shall be
entitled to an equitable adjustment for Work performed prior to termination,
including a reasonable profit, but in no event shall Supplier's recovery
exceed the Purchase Order price for terminated items.


================================================================================
                    SECTION 2: QUALITY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

2.1 QUALITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

Supplier shall maintain a Quality Management System (QMS) certified to
AS9100 Rev D (or later revision) by an accredited registrar recognized by
the International Aerospace Quality Group (IAQG). Supplier shall provide
evidence of current certification to SDC upon request and shall notify SDC
within five (5) business days of any suspension, withdrawal, conditional
status, or scope limitation of its AS9100 certification.

Supplier's QMS shall address, at a minimum, the requirements of AS9100 Rev D
clauses 4 through 10 as applicable to the scope of work performed under this
Purchase Order.

2.2 FIRST ARTICLE INSPECTION

First Article Inspection (FAI) in accordance with AS9102 is required for
Line 001 (Flight Critical) prior to commencement of production deliveries.
Partial or delta FAI may be acceptable for Lines 002 and 003 where the
Supplier can demonstrate prior FAI on identical or similar configurations.

The First Article Inspection Report (FAIR) shall include:
    (a) AS9102 Form 1 — Part Number Accountability
    (b) AS9102 Form 2 — Product Accountability (material, special processes,
        functional testing)
    (c) AS9102 Form 3 — Characteristic Accountability (all drawing
        dimensions and tolerances with actual measured values)
    (d) Supporting objective evidence (CMM reports, test data, material
        certs, NDT results)

The FAIR shall be submitted to SDC Quality Assurance a minimum of fifteen
(15) business days prior to the first production shipment. Production
deliveries shall not commence until SDC has provided written approval.

2.3 SOURCE INSPECTION REQUIREMENTS

Source inspection by SDC quality representatives is required for Line 001
(Flight Critical) prior to shipment, in accordance with SDC-Q-100 Rev C.
SDC reserves the right to perform source inspection on Lines 002 and 003
at its discretion.

Source inspection shall include but not be limited to:
    (a) Review of manufacturing records and traveler documentation
    (b) Dimensional verification of critical characteristics
    (c) Witness of functional testing per the applicable test procedure
    (d) Review of material certifications and special process records
    (e) Verification of configuration and serialization

Supplier shall provide SDC Quality Assurance a minimum of five (5) business
days advance notice prior to any source inspection hold point. Supplier
shall not ship source-inspected items without SDC quality representative
sign-off on the source inspection record.

Contact for source inspection scheduling:
    Maria Vasquez, Supplier Quality Engineer
    maria.vasquez@stellardynamics.com
    (256) 555-7145

2.4 WIRE HARNESS REQUIREMENTS (LINE 001)

The wire harness assembly on Line 001 shall be manufactured in accordance
with SDC-Q-250 Rev A, Electrical Wiring and Harnessing Standards. In
addition, the following requirements apply:

    (a) All wiring shall conform to IPC/WHMA-A-620 Class 3
    (b) Crimped terminations per NASA-STD-8739.4
    (c) 100% continuity and hipot testing per SDC-Q-250 Rev A Section 4.0
    (d) ESD protection per ANSI/ESD S20.20 throughout manufacturing,
        test, storage, and shipping
    (e) Wire marking per MIL-STD-202, Method 215

All wire harness production and inspection personnel shall hold current
IPC/WHMA-A-620 certification. Evidence of personnel certifications shall
be maintained and provided upon request.

2.5 NONCONFORMING MATERIAL CONTROL

Supplier shall not ship any nonconforming material without prior written
disposition from SDC. Deviation or waiver requests shall be submitted
through the SDC Supplier Quality Portal and shall include:

    (a) Detailed description of the nonconformance including affected
        characteristics and actual versus required values
    (b) Root cause analysis
    (c) Corrective and preventive action plan
    (d) Proposed disposition (use-as-is, rework, repair, or scrap)
    (e) Engineering justification for use-as-is or repair dispositions
    (f) Quantity and serial numbers of affected units

Nonconforming material shipped without authorization may be returned at
Supplier's expense and shall constitute grounds for corrective action
against the Supplier.

2.6 CORRECTIVE ACTION REQUIREMENTS

Supplier shall maintain a corrective action system conforming to AS9100
Rev D requirements. Upon notification by SDC of a quality escape or
customer complaint, Supplier shall:

    (a) Respond with containment actions within twenty-four (24) hours
    (b) Perform suspect lot analysis and provide containment scope
        within forty-eight (48) hours
    (c) Submit root cause analysis within ten (10) business days using
        structured problem-solving methodology (8D, A3, or equivalent)
    (d) Submit corrective action plan with implementation timeline
        within fifteen (15) business days
    (e) Provide objective evidence of corrective action effectiveness
        within sixty (60) days of implementation

Failure to respond to corrective action requests in a timely manner may
result in escalated supplier surveillance, restriction to the SDC
Supplier Watch List, or removal from the Approved Supplier List.


================================================================================
              SECTION 3: MATERIAL AND TRACEABILITY
================================================================================

3.1 MATERIAL CERTIFICATION AND TRACEABILITY

All materials used in the manufacture of Goods under this Purchase Order
shall be traceable to the original material manufacturer. Supplier shall
maintain material traceability in accordance with SDC-Q-100 Rev C
Section 4.0 documentation requirements.

For Line 002 (titanium mounting bracket), material procurement and
processing shall additionally comply with SDC-M-030 v4.1, Material and
Process Requirements for Metallic Components. Specifically:

    (a) Titanium alloy (Ti-6Al-4V per AMS 4911) shall be procured from
        sources listed on the SDC Approved Material Supplier List
    (b) Certified Mill Test Reports (CMTR) per SDC-M-030 v4.1
        Section 2.0 shall be provided for all metallic raw materials
    (c) Heat treatment per AMS 2801 with Nadcap-accredited processor
    (d) Material traceability from raw stock through finished part

Material certifications shall be retained by the Supplier for a minimum
of fifteen (15) years from the date of delivery.

3.2 COUNTERFEIT PARTS PREVENTION

Supplier shall maintain a Counterfeit Parts Prevention Program conforming
to AS6174 and SAE AS6496 requirements. Supplier shall:

    (a) Procure parts only from Original Component Manufacturers (OCM),
        Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEM), or authorized distributors
    (b) Maintain an internal approved supplier list with verification of
        authorized distribution agreements
    (c) Perform incoming inspection appropriate to detect counterfeit
        parts per AS6171 methodology
    (d) Maintain full traceability of electronic and electrical
        components to their source
    (e) Quarantine and report suspected counterfeit parts to SDC and
        GIDEP within thirty (30) days of discovery

3.3 SPECIAL PROCESS CONTROL

All special processes performed on Goods under this Purchase Order shall
be performed by Nadcap-accredited processors or by the Supplier if
Supplier maintains current Nadcap accreditation for the applicable process.
Special processes include but are not limited to:

    (a) Heat treatment (all metallic alloys)
    (b) Chemical processing (anodizing, plating, chemical conversion)
    (c) Nondestructive testing (ultrasonic, radiographic, penetrant,
        magnetic particle)
    (d) Welding (electron beam, TIG, resistance)
    (e) Shot peening
    (f) Crimping and soldering (per MIL-STD-1520 requirements)

Supplier shall maintain current Nadcap certificates for all special
processes performed in-house and shall provide copies to SDC upon request.
Changes to special process sources require prior written approval from SDC.

3.4 PROHIBITED AND RESTRICTED MATERIALS

Materials and substances prohibited or restricted by SDC-M-030 v4.1
Section 5.0 shall not be used in any Goods delivered under this Purchase
Order unless a specific waiver has been granted by SDC Materials
Engineering. The Supplier shall provide material declarations upon request
identifying any substances of concern per EU REACH Regulation and SDC
prohibited materials list.

4.2 PACKAGING AND PRESERVATION

All Goods shall be packaged to prevent damage from handling, transportation,

    (a) Line 001 (wire harness): ESD-protective packaging per ANSI/ESD

Each package shall be marked with:
    (a) SDC Purchase Order number
    (b) SDC Part Number and revision

    (d) Serial number or lot number
    (e) Manufacturing date
    (f) Country of Origin

4.3 SHIPPING DOCUMENTATION

Each shipment shall include the following documentation:

    (a) Packing slip with Purchase Order number, line item, part number,
        quantity, and serial/lot numbers
    (b) Certificate of Conformance (CoC) signed by an authorized quality
        representative certifying that all Goods conform to Purchase Order
        requirements
    (c) Material certifications as required by Section 3.1
    (d) First Article Inspection Report (for initial shipments of FAI-
        required items, if not previously submitted)
    (e) Test data and inspection reports as required by the applicable
        drawings and specifications
    (f) Special process certifications (heat treat, NDT, plating, etc.)
    (g) Country of Origin declaration

Documentation shall be submitted electronically via the SDC Supplier
Quality Portal concurrent with shipment. Hard copies shall be included
in the shipping container.
